Cuca Italiana

Description

Cuca Italiana is a Brazillian dish traditional of the South of the country. The dish is made with a sweet and buttery dough, covered on top by crunchy farofa.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 120ml of orange juice
  • 120ml of water
  • 240ml of milk
  • 170g of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of sweetener
  • 3 tablespoons of dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 3 tablespoons of heavy cream
  • 3 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1kg of wheat flour
  • grated nutmeg to taste
  • fennel to taste

Ingredients for the crunchy farofa

  • 3/4 a cup of wheat flour
  • 3/4 a cup of sugar
  • 4 tablespoons of butter
  • Cinnamon to taste
  • 1 egg
  • Half a cup of vanilla essence

Steps

  1. In a large bowl put the two eggs, the orange juice, water, the milk, sugar, the sweetener, yeast, cooking oil, butter, heavy cream, grated nutmeg and the fennel.
  2. Mix the ingredients in the bowl, then put two cups of wheat flour and mix again, add the salt and mix once again.
  3. Keep adding wheat flour until the ingredients transform into a sticky dough.
  4. Spread wheat flour on a table and knead the dough oh that table for 15 minutes, if necessary add more wheat flour.
  5. When the dough start getting smooth put it in a bowl, cover it, and let it rest for 40 minutes.
  6. Put the dough in a clean table, and separate the dough in six parts.
  7. Make long rolls with the dough. With 3 of these rools, braid them together, and repeat with the other 3.
  8. Put the braided dough in a mould, cover it and let it ferment for 1h and 15 minutes, by the end it should be double the size.
  9. In a small bowl put the ingredients e scrub with a finger until you get a thick and uniform farofa.
  10. Whip the egg with the vanilla essence.
  11. Brush the whipped mixture over the fermented dough, and spread the farova across the entire superfice of the brushed dough.
  12. Take it to bake in a preheated oven at 200Â°C until the dough is golden brown.
